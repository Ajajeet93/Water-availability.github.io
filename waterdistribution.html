GLOBAL WATER DISTIBUTION:

Global water distribution refers to the unequal distribution of Earth's freshwater resources across the planet. While water covers about 71% of the Earth's surface, only a small percentage of it is readily available as freshwater. The majority of freshwater is locked in ice caps and glaciers, and the remaining accessible freshwater is found in groundwater and surface water sources.
This uneven distribution poses challenges as some regions face severe water scarcity, while others have more abundant water resources. International cooperation and responsible water management are essential for ensuring equitable access to clean water and addressing global water challenges. The sustainable and responsible use of available water resources is vital for securing a prosperous and resilient future for communities and the environment.
