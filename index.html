WATER AVAILABILITY

INTRODUCTION

Water availability refers to the presence of a reliable and sufficient supply of freshwater
resources in a specific region or area. It is a fundamental concept critical for sustaining life, ecosystems, and various human activities. Water availability encompasses the accessibility of clean and usable water for drinking, sanitation, irrigation, industrial processes, and ecological balance. This essential resource is indispensable for human health, agriculture, industrial productivity, and the preservation of biodiversity. Factors influencing water availability include climate, geography, population, pollution, and responsible management practices. Securing water availability is imperative for addressing global water challenges and ensuring a resilient and
prosperous future for communities and the environment.

WATER SOURCES

Water sources refer to the origins of freshwater that sustain life, ecosystems, and human activities.These sources include surface water like rivers and lakes, groundwater found in aquifers, rainwater collected through harvesting.

GLOBAL WATER DISTIBUTION

Global water distribution refers to the unequal distribution of Earth's freshwater resources across the planet. While water covers about 71% of the Earth's surface, only a small percentage of it is readily available as freshwater.
